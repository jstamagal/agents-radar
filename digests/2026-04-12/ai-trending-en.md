### 3. Trend Signal Analysis

**The "Agent Harness" Wars Have Begun**
While 2025 was about proving agents could code, 2026 is shaping up to be about *how we manage them*. The data reveals a massive surge in interest for **"Agent Harnesses" and "Methodologies"**. Repos like `Archon`, `obra/superpowers`, and `multica` are not just running agents; they are building infrastructure to track, verify, and reproduce agent behavior. This is a direct response to the stochastic nature of LLMs—developers are now treating AI coding as a supply chain problem that needs deterministic quality assurance.

**"Claude Code" Optimization as a Genre**
The presence of `andrej-karpathy-skills`, `claude-code-best-practice`, and `everything-claude-code` indicates a market shift. Instead of just generic prompts, developers are building specific "System 2" wrappers (instincts, skills, memory compression) around Anthropic's models. The community is effectively reverse-engineering the "perfect coding engineer" by curating prompt chains that enforce rigorous testing and planning before code generation.

**Data Ingestion Remains the Hardest Problem**
Despite advanced models, `Microsoft/markitdown` and `opendataloader-pdf` are exploding in popularity. This signals that the "last mile" of AI—getting unstructured corporate data (PDFs, PPTs) into a vector format—remains a major pain point. The trend is moving away from generic RAG chatbots toward specialized "ETL for AI" pipelines that prioritize metadata preservation and accessibility compliance.
