## 3. Trend Signal Analysis

**The Rise of the "Agent Harness"**
The most explosive growth today is not in generic LLMs, but in the *tooling layer* that makes models useful. The massive star counts for **everything-claude-code** and **deer-flow** signal a shift: developers are moving past "prompt engineering" to **Agent Engineering**. The community is racing to build "harnesses"—systems that provide memory, skills, and security isolation to models like Claude 3.5/4.

**Vertical Agents (Finance & Security)**
Generalist agents are giving way to specialized vertical agents. **TradingAgents** and **Pentagi** highlight a trend where autonomous systems are being deployed in high-stakes environments (financial markets and cybersecurity). These projects often utilize a multi-agent architecture (one agent to research, one to execute, one to critique) to handle complex workflows.

**The "Computer Use" Stack**
With projects like **browser-use**, **trycua**, and **Project N.O.M.A.D** trending, it is clear that the "Computer Use" paradigm (AI controlling the OS/Browser) is the defining UI trend of early 2026. The focus is on **Sandboxing** and **Observability**—ensuring these agents can execute actions safely and recall their history.
